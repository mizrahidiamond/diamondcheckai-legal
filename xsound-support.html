XSound — Support

Control your Bose SoundTouch speaker over WiFi.

Quick Start

  1. Make sure your Bose SoundTouch speaker is powered on and connected to the same WiFi network as your iPhone.
  2. Open XSound. It will auto-discover the speaker.
  3. Tap the speaker name to connect.
  4. Browse stations, pick a preset, or control playback from the Remote tab.

No Bose speaker? Tap "Listen on iPhone" on the first screen to stream radio through your iPhone, Bluetooth, or AirPlay device.

Frequently Asked Questions

My speaker isn't discovered.
Make sure XSound has Local Network permission (Settings → XSound → Local Network). The speaker must be on the same WiFi network as your iPhone and powered on. Try pulling down to refresh the scan.

Re-connecting Your Speaker to WiFi

If you changed your WiFi password, got a new router, or moved, your Bose SoundTouch speaker needs to be re-paired to the network. XSound v1.0 doesn't yet include a setup flow (coming in a future update), but you can re-pair manually in under 2 minutes:

Step 1 — Enter WiFi setup mode

  • SoundTouch speakers (10, 20, 30, Portable): Simultaneously press and hold the "2" and Volume Down (−) buttons on top of the speaker for about 5 seconds, until the WiFi indicator glows solid amber.
  • SoundTouch soundbars: Press and hold the "9" button until the lights flash.

Step 2 — Connect and configure

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings → WiFi. You should see a new network called BoseSoundTouch XXXX (where XXXX is a 4-character code). Join it.
  2. Open Safari and go to http://192.168.1.1. The speaker's setup page will load.
  3. Enter your home WiFi network name and the new password. Submit.
  4. Wait ~30 seconds. The speaker will reboot and join your home WiFi.
  5. Reconnect your iPhone to your normal home WiFi, then open XSound. The speaker should appear under "Recently Connected" or be auto-discovered.

Alternative — Old Bose SoundTouch app (if still installed)

If you still have the official Bose SoundTouch app installed, its WiFi setup feature is local-network-only and still works (even though the cloud service is gone). Open it, choose "Set Up New Speaker," and follow the prompts.

Factory reset (last resort)

If nothing else works, factory reset the speaker by holding the Power and Volume Down (−) buttons for 10 seconds, then re-pair using the steps above.

A one-tap speaker setup flow is coming to XSound in v1.1.

My presets disappeared.
Bose ended their cloud service on May 6, 2026. Presets that were cloud-synced may have been lost when the cloud shut down. To restore them in XSound: play a station, go to the Remote tab, long-press any preset button, and tap "Save Now Playing to Preset." This saves the station directly to your speaker's local storage — it will never disappear again.

Nothing happens when I tap play.
If you're connected to a Bose speaker, make sure the speaker is powered on and not in standby. If you're using iPhone playback mode, check that your device isn't muted and that the station has a valid stream (some stations go offline).

I hear Spanish stations when I tap the Paris tile.
That was fixed in v1.0 — Paris, Tel Aviv, Tokyo, and Miami now use TuneIn's city-specific region IDs for accurate local results.

Can I switch between Bose and iPhone playback?
Yes. On the Now Playing tab, tap the Output pill below the volume slider. When playing through iPhone, you can route to any Bluetooth or AirPlay device using the AirPlay icon.

Does XSound work after Bose's cloud shutdown?
Yes. XSound communicates directly with your speaker over your local WiFi network. It doesn't depend on Bose's cloud service at all. As long as your WiFi router runs, your speaker keeps working.

What speakers are supported?
All Bose SoundTouch models including SoundTouch 10, 20, 30, Portable, and the Wave series. Anything that worked with the original Bose SoundTouch app.

Does the app collect any data?
No. XSound collects zero data, has no accounts, no analytics, and no third-party SDKs. See the Privacy Policy.
